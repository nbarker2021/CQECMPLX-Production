# Paper 21.75 - MorphForge Next-State Bridge

## Bridge Role

Paper 21 exports an applied-reader discipline to the next papers. It does not
export unrestricted proof. The receiving paper gets a verified way to read,
encode, account, and place an observation; the receiving paper must still prove
its own domain result.

## Exported Artifacts

The next state receives:

- the lossless ribbon requirement,
- the S3 fold classifier,
- the morphon ledger schema,
- the explicit open-gap taxonomy,
- the 24-dimensional terminal landing template,
- the hidden-guess diagnostic pattern for non-math validation.

## Legal Use

A later paper may map a material, protein, glyph, calendar item, recipe object,
CAD wireframe, or game state into the MorphForge reader. It may then state that
the object has been read, encoded, accounted, and placed. To state that the
object has been solved, optimized, simulated, manufactured, or experimentally
confirmed, the later paper must attach a domain verifier.

## Illegal Use

A later paper may not claim material closure, protein closure, CAD closure,
SolidWorks correctness, chess closure, biological closure, TF1 closure, or Leech
closure from Paper 21 alone. Those claims are allowed only as open bridges until
their own receipts are present.

## Preconditioned Next State

The next paper begins with a cleaner entry condition: the object must first pass
the Paper 21 reader. If it cannot be made into a replayable ribbon and an
auditable morphon row, then it is not yet ready for a stronger scientific claim.
If it can, the later verifier can focus on the domain behavior instead of
rebuilding the observation-accounting layer from scratch.
